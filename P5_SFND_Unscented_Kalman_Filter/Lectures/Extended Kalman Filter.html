How to Perform a Taylor Expansion

The general form of a Taylor series expansion of an equation, f(x)f(x)f(x), at point \mu is as follows:

f(x)f()+f()x(x)f(x) \approx f(\mu) + \frac{\partial f(\mu)}{\partial x} ( x - \mu)f(x)f()+xf()(x)

Simply replace f(x)f(x)f(x) with a given equation, find the partial derivative, and plug in the value \mu to find the Taylor expansion at that value of \mu.
